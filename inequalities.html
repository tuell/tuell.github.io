The Inequalities

Six different categories of inequality are listed on each card - here ist more information on the categories and on how the data were collected.

Carbon footprint

The carbon footprint states the yearly amount of carbon dioxide emissions in tons per person. The data is from 2013 and was collected and calculated by the Emissions Database for Atmospheric Research (EDGAR), a project of the European Commission - see the data.

Cost of living

The cost of living is symbolized by the price of one liter of milk in the respective country. This statistic was chosen because milk is being consumed almost everywhere by almost everyone. The data comes from numbeo.com and is available here.

Freedom of the press

This statistic represents the place of each country in the Freedom of Press-Ranking of Reporters Without Borders. The ranking is available here.

Internet access

This number lists the percentage of the population that can access the internet from their home. The data was collected by InternetLiveStats.com.

Life expectancy

This statistic shows how old people in the respective country get on average. The data was collected by the World Health Organization.

Women's vote

This category lists the year in which women got equal voting rights in comparison with men on the national level. The years were taken from this Wikipedia page.
